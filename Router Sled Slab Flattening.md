Build plans for a Router Sled for Slab flattening. Must accommodate a 3’ X 8’ hardwood slab. Should accommodate up to 4” thick slab

Y-Frame: 2x3 rectangular steel tube, 95” long, 14 Gauge connect to 2x4 lumber at right-angles at ends to create a box. Connection is flat plates (2x4 trimmed at ends to 2x3 to fit). Steel plates connect to Y-Tube using M6 Rivnut, then to 2x4 using wood screws. Y Linear Rails Connect to frame via M6 Rivnuts (30 Rivnuts required per rail)

X-Gantry assembly: Two 2x3 inch 14g steel tube, 40"  base. 1000MM Linear Rails attached with M6 Rivnuts and Socket head cap screws. Acrylic Router Base, attached to Ball Bearing Rollers using Z-Brackets 16" extra 2x3 tube may be used by cutting in 8 inch pieces to frame out the gantry.

Note: The 2x3 14G steel was purchased as a 24’ tube, it has been cut by the vendor in two 96" and two 40”, leaving a 16" leftover piece. In addition, these pieces have all been painted using latex enamel paint.

The entire unit should be able to be disassembled for storage. It will be assembled on a cement floor, and must be adjustable to level
